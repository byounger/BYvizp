Visualization Story: Income Wealth Gap


STATIC

by Brittney Younger

This visualization was developed as an improvement for a report. The report originally included two line graphs that depicted the wealth gap between the top 5%, next 7%, next 40%, and the bottom 50% for US families and the wealth gap between specific racial demographics. When considering the improvements, my main goal was to find simplistic ways to capture trends, the major gaps and highlight differences between each groups without being overpowering. I really wanted to think outside the box to provide a visual that was exploratory as well as informative.



BEFORE

AFTER



First Line Graph – I decided to move a way from a line graph and come up with a new way to illustrate trends through individual heat maps. Each heat map equals an income group and each box in the heat map equals a year. The color of the heat maps is based on the income range.



Second Line Graph – Due to space constraints and amount of variables illustrated, I decided to stick with the line graph, but split them into two graphs. Both graphs are practically identical except for the African American and Latino indicators. Using Excel, I colored in the gap between the two lines for each graphic and calculated the growth/decline difference between the racial groups for additional annotation.
